Score the composition and visual clarity of a single cat picture. Evaluate how well the photograph is framed, whether the cat is in focus, and how effectively the lighting and contrast present the subject. A picture with strong natural composition that draws the eye toward the cat — free from cluttered backgrounds and distracting elements — should receive a high score. Technical perfection is less important than intentional framing and the ability of the viewer to immediately see and appreciate the cat without visual confusion. Consider the following factors: Is the cat in focus and clearly visible? Does the framing draw attention naturally toward the cat? Is the lighting effective — neither overexposed nor lost in shadow? Is the background clean and non-distracting? Is there a sense of visual order and intentionality in the composition? A slightly grainy phone photo with strong natural composition should score higher than a high-resolution image that is poorly framed. The score should reflect how well the image works as a photograph, with the cat as its intended subject.
